Lugaw

Lugaw, also known as lugao, is a sticky rice porridge or dish from the Philippines.
Lugaw can be used to describe a variety of savory and sweet foods.
Savory lugaw are called to as pospas in Visayan regions.
In the Philippines, lugaw is commonly considered a comfort dish.

Ingredients Required

    1 cup long grain white rice
    4 to 5 cups water
    2 teaspoons salt
    1/4 cup rousong pork floss

Cooking Method

  • Pour water in a cooking pot. Bring to a boil.
  • Put-in the rice. Continue cooking for 30 minutes or until the texture becomes thick, while stirring once in awhile.
  • Add the salt, stir and then cook for 2 minutes more.
  • Transfer to a serving bowl. Top with a tablespoon of rousing.
  • Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
