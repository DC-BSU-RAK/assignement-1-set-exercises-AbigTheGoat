Lugaw

Lugaw, also known as lugao, is a sticky rice porridge or dish from the Philippines.
Lugaw can be used to describe a variety of savory and sweet foods.
Savory lugaw are called to as pospas in Visayan regions.
In the Philippines, lugaw is commonly considered a comfort dish.

Ingredients Required

    1 cup long grain white rice
    4 to 5 cups water
    2 teaspoons salt
    1/4 cup rousong pork floss

Cooking Method

  1. Pour water in a cooking pot and bring to a boil.
  2. Add the rice and cook for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add salt, stir, and cook for 2 more minutes.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl and top with 1 tablespoon of rousong.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
